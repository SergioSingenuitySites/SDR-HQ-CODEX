Agritainment & farm experiences ★ your passion area
Corn mazes farm
Inland, rural midwest/southeast
 Q1–Q2 Sep – Nov (fall peak) Online pre-sale + day-of gate POS is exactly the split they deal with. Priority booking fills timed entry slots. Cash flow matters hugely for small family farms.
U-pick farms farm
Strawberry, blueberry, peach, apple
 Q2 Jun – Oct (crop-dependent) Timed entry + add-on sales (jam, cider, merchandise) = perfect POS + booking combo. Many still on pen-and-paper or Square alone.
Agritourism / farm tours farm
Regenerative, organic, homestead-style
 Q1–Q2 Apr – Oct (planting/harvest) CSA pickups, farm dinners, school tours — multi-event booking + retail POS for farm store. Deep dive candidate. Fast-growing, underserved by tech.
Lavender / flower farms farm
Midwest, Pacific Northwest, Texas Hill Country
 Q2 Jun – Aug (bloom season) Sell tickets + sell bouquets/products in one system. Very few use real booking software — huge greenfield opportunity.
Pumpkin patches farm
Often bundled with corn mazes
 Q1–Q2 Sep – Nov Timed entry, hayride slots, gift shop POS. Talk to them in spring before they re-sign with anyone else. Same playbook as corn mazes.
Winery / vineyard tours farm
Inland — VA, TX Hill Country, Finger Lakes, MO
 Q2–Q3 Sep – Nov (harvest/crush) Tasting room POS + tour booking is a real pain point. Many use Fareharbor but hate the cash flow delay. Merchant-of-record angle is very strong here.
Outdoor adventure (inland)
Ziplines
Appalachians, Ozarks, Smokies, Rockies
 Q1–Q2 May – Sep (summer peak) Singenuity was literally born at ClimbWorks (zipline). You can speak their language. Priority booking to fill lines before opening new slots = big staffing win.
Ropes courses / aerial parks
 Q1–Q2 May – Sep Same operational pain as ziplines. Waivers, timed slots, gift shop — needs combined POS + booking. Easy conversation starter with the ClimbWorks origin story.
Whitewater rafting / river guides
Nantahala, Ocoee, Arkansas River, etc.
 Q1–Q2 May – Aug Slot-based tours + gear rental POS + photo/merch sales. Very ad-heavy operators — marketing attribution pitch is compelling for them.
Cavern / cave tours
Missouri, Kentucky, Tennessee, Virginia
 Q1 May – Sep (summer tourism) Year-round ops but summer is slammed. Call NOW. Gift shop + timed tour tickets in one system. Often family-owned, underserved by tech.
Rock climbing gyms / outdoor guides
 Q2 Jun – Sep Membership + day pass POS + guide booking. Priority booking to fill beginner slots. Understated but solid fit.
Horseback riding / trail rides
Rural, inland — great Fareharbor competitor angle
 Q1–Q2 Apr – Oct Often on Fareharbor but small enough to care about fee difference. Cash flow pitch is very strong. Usually no real POS at all — easy upgrade conversation.
ATV / off-road tour operators
 Q1–Q2 Apr – Oct Gear rental + tour booking combo. Often family-run, cash-heavy, no real software. Great greenfield. Midwest, Southeast, Southwest.
Hot air balloon operators
Albuquerque, Midwest plains, Texas Hill Country
 Q1–Q2 Spring – fall (weather-dependent) Slot-based, rescheduling-heavy. High-ticket experience operators spend a lot on ads — marketing attribution is a killer pitch. Champagne/gift POS adds up.
Hunting / fishing outfitters
South, Midwest, Mountain West
 Q1–Q2 Fall hunting season + spring fishing opener Multi-day trip booking + gear/license retail POS. Most on outdated systems or nothing. Strong cash flow and low-fee pitch. Very inland audience.
Nature / wildlife tour guides
Birding, wildflower, foliage tours
 Q1–Q2 Spring – fall peak viewing seasons Small passionate operators, very underserved by tech. Easy conversation. Booking + branded merchandise is simple but real pain point for them.
Inland paddling / canoe / kayak outfitters
Rivers and lakes — not coast
 Q2 May – Sep Rental POS + guided tour booking + shuttle logistics. Similar to your coast calls but inland. Different decision-makers, less rep competition.
Cultural & educational attractions
Museums (small-to-mid size)
Science, history, children's — inland cities
 Year-round Summer + school breaks Gift shop POS + timed entry + membership. Combined reporting is a major differentiator vs. their current patchwork systems.
Aquariums (regional / inland)
 Year-round Summer + school breaks Same as museums but add programming/class bookings. Priority booking for limited-capacity events is key pitch.
Escape rooms
 Year-round Weekends + holidays (call weekday mornings) Pure slot-based booking, often on generic software. Priority booking + marketing attribution is a slam dunk. Low seasonality = easy to reach anytime.
Axe throwing / entertainment venues
 Year-round Fri/Sat nights, holidays Slot booking + bar/food POS. Very few use purpose-built tour software. Fast-growing segment, very receptive to better tools.
Mini golf / putting courses
Seasonal in north, year-round in south
 Year-round / Q1 north May – Sep (north) POS for snacks/merch + timed entry. Bayview Mini Putt is literally on your website — easy demo comparison to ClimbWorks booking flow.
Indoor climbing gyms
 Year-round No extreme seasonality Day pass + membership + class booking + gear rental POS. Combined system is a real pain point for them right now.
Holiday & seasonal events
Holiday light displays
Botanical gardens, drive-throughs, walk-throughs
 Q3 (Jul–Sep) Nov – Jan Timed entry + hot cocoa/merch POS = perfect pitch. Call in summer when they're planning. Often run by nonprofits or parks — very underserved.
Haunted attractions / Halloween events
 Q3 (Jul–Aug) Sep – Nov Slot-based timed entry, merchandise, add-ons. Priority booking to fill early slots first. Very ad-heavy — marketing attribution is a killer pitch.
Renaissance fairs / festival operators
 Q2 Varies — mostly spring/fall Multi-day event booking + vendor/retail POS. Complex ops our system handles well. Often large enough to really care about reporting quality.
Winter / cold weather (inland)
Ski resorts / snow tubing parks
Mid-size, not major resorts
 Q2–Q3 Dec – Mar Lift ticket POS + lesson booking + rental POS + lodge retail — massive combined-system pain point. Priority booking for lesson slots is very compelling.
Dog sledding / snowmobile tours
Minnesota, Michigan UP, Colorado, Wyoming
 Q2–Q3 Dec – Mar Small operators, often no real booking software. Tour slots + merch POS. Easy Fareharbor/pen-and-paper replacement conversation.
Ice fishing guide services
 Q2–Q3 Dec – Mar (ice season) Booking + gear rental + bait/tackle POS. Very underserved. Probably on Venmo and paper right now. Strong cash flow pitch.
